# SPEC-018 Risk Register: Dialectica Universalis

| # | Claim | Status | Risk | Severity | Mitigation |
|---|-------|--------|------|----------|------------|
| R1 | Each ORGANVM organ IS a distinct dialect of universal logic | GROUNDED | Over-metaphor: mapping may be analogical, not structural | HIGH | Require at least one formal isomorphism proof per organ pair at Tier 1; flag "analogy" vs "isomorphism" explicitly in taxonomy.py |
| R2 | The Curry-Howard-Lambek correspondence extends to all eight organs | SPECULATIVE for non-core pairs | Classical CHC covers logic↔types↔categories, not art↔governance↔community | HIGH | Define precisely which correspondences are formal (CHC core: I↔III) and which are structural extensions. Do not claim formal proof where none exists. The 4-tier system (FORMAL/STRUCTURAL/ANALOGICAL/EMERGENT) enforces this |
| R3 | Inter-organ translations are structure-preserving (isomorphisms) | GROUNDED for I↔III, SPECULATIVE for II↔VI | Translations may lose information — may be homomorphisms, not isomorphisms | MEDIUM | Conway 1968: organizational↔system mapping is homomorphism (projection), not always isomorphism. Classify each translation by PreservationDegree. 4 levels prevent false precision |
| R4 | The system can prove its own unity computationally | GROUNDED | Requires formal definitions of "structural correspondence" that are mechanically checkable | MEDIUM | Use existing engine patterns: detector.py has 4 correspondence types with measurable strength (0.0–1.0). False positives filtered by FALS-003 vacuity criterion |
| R5 | The liberal arts tradition is a valid historical antecedent | HISTORICAL | Risk of false genealogy — mapping modern system theory onto medieval categories | LOW | Cite primary sources (Hugh of Saint-Victor, Boethius). Flag differences between historical intuition and formal proof. The RESEARCH document cites 43 primary texts |
| R6 | Unfalsifiability: thesis may be too broad to disprove | CRITICAL | If everything is a "dialect," the claim becomes vacuous | CRITICAL | Define concrete falsification criteria (FALS-001 through FALS-003). If the detector cannot discriminate correspondence from non-correspondence, the thesis is vacuous and SPEC-018 must be revised |
| R7 | Scope creep: claiming all knowledge is one knowledge | HIGH | The thesis may be extended to domains where it doesn't hold | MEDIUM | Restrict to the eight organs as defined. Emergent (Tier 4) pairs are explicitly marked as "not yet characterized" — they are hypotheses, not claims |
| R8 | Implementation conflation: the code IS the thesis | MEDIUM | Risk that the trivium module is treated as proof rather than as a detection instrument | MEDIUM | The module detects and classifies correspondences — it does not create them. The formal proofs (Curry-Howard, etc.) exist independently of the code |
