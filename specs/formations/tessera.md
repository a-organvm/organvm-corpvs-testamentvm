## 2. Trigger Conditions

TESSERA activates when:
- Principal must interface with a formal system (landlord, employer, court, bank, government agency, university)
- A new external relationship is initiated
- Identity coherence audit triggered (scheduled or by representative detecting inconsistency)
- PRAXIS hands off a new client/employer relationship for ongoing management
- AEGIS requires formal communication with a threatening entity
- Manual invocation by principal ("I need to present myself to X")

## 3. Primitive Roles

| Primitive | Role in TESSERA | Frame |
|-----------|----------------|-------|
| registrar | Entry point — establishes current formal standing across all external relationships | formal_standing |
| representative | Presents consistent, strategically calibrated identity to the specific entity | identity_presentation |
| advocate | Prepares the principal's position for the specific interaction (not adversarial — strategic) | position_preparation |
| liaison | Maintains the ongoing relationship channel after initial engagement | relationship_maintenance |
| insulator | Compartmentalizes — ensures information appropriate to one relationship doesn't leak to another | information_compartment |

## 5. Escalation Policy

| Primitive | Escalation Rule |
|-----------|----------------|
| registrar | Escalates if standing is lapsed, revoked, or disputed |
| representative | Escalates for first contact with a new entity OR for any adversarial entity |
| advocate | Escalates if position requires claims the principal hasn't verified |
| liaison | Escalates if relationship health deteriorates (signals: non-response, hostility, unexpected demands) |
| insulator | Escalates if compartment breach is detected (information leaked between contexts) |

## 6. Exit Conditions

- Relationship stabilized: liaison reports steady-state for 90+ days with no escalation
- Relationship terminated: entity engagement is closed (contract ended, account closed, dispute resolved)
- Upgraded to AEGIS: if the relationship becomes adversarial, TESSERA hands off to AEGIS for defensive posture
- Downgraded to dormant: no active signal exchange; liaison enters minimal-contact maintenance mode

## 7. Why TESSERA Crystallizes Fourth

The first three formations (AEGIS, OIKONOMIA, PRAXIS) generate actions that require interfacing with external entities:
- AEGIS triggers legal communications
- OIKONOMIA triggers benefit applications and creditor negotiations
- PRAXIS triggers client/employer engagement

TESSERA is the formation that ensures all these external interfaces are **coherent and strategically managed** rather than ad hoc. Without TESSERA, each formation handles its own external communication — creating identity fragmentation and information leakage. With TESSERA, every external interface passes through a unified identity management formation.

## 8. INST-FORMATION Classification

**Formation type**: INTERFACE (boundary-spanning — manages the system's relationship with its environment, translating between internal state and external presentation).

**Interdependence profile**: Bidirectional with all other formations (receives engagement requests from AEGIS, OIKONOMIA, PRAXIS; returns relationship state). This is architecturally significant: TESSERA is the universal external membrane.

## 9. Relationship to SPEC-019 / PERSONA

TESSERA is the institutional formation of identity management. It relates to but is distinct from the PERSONA organ (4444J99, digital masks):
- PERSONA creates and maintains identity artifacts (portfolio, profiles, public presence)
- TESSERA manages the strategic deployment of identity in institutional contexts

PERSONA is production (making identity artifacts). TESSERA is institutional (deploying them strategically). They compose as hybrid formations when needed.
