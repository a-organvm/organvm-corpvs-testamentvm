## 1. Composition Graph

```
assessor(market + capabilities) → strategist(positioning) → appraiser(services) → negotiator
          ↑                                                                            ↓
    archivist (past work, outcomes)                                               incorporator (contract/entity structure)
                                                                                       ↓
                                                                                  insulator (liability separation)
```

**Operators used**: CHAIN (→), with archivist feeding assessor (precedent), and incorporator + insulator as post-negotiation structural chain.

## 2. Trigger Conditions

PRAXIS activates when:
- OIKONOMIA reports income below sustainability threshold
- Current income sources are unstable or declining
- A new opportunity is identified that requires positioning/negotiation
- Manual invocation by principal ("I need to generate income" or "evaluate this opportunity")
- Strategic repositioning is desired (not crisis-driven, but growth-driven)

## 3. Primitive Roles

| Primitive | Role in PRAXIS | Frame |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| assessor | Evaluates principal's capabilities against market demand, informed by historical performance | market_capabilities |
| strategist | Identifies optimal positioning — not "what jobs to apply for" but "what configuration commands highest return" | positional_advantage |
| appraiser | Sets pricing for the positioned offering | service_valuation |
| negotiator | Handles the exchange — compensation, terms, scope | deal_negotiation |
| incorporator | Structures the relationship (W-2, 1099, entity-to-entity, project-based) | contract_structure |
| insulator | Ensures liability separation between the principal and the engagement | liability_compartment |
| archivist | Provides historical data — what worked, what didn't, what the market responded to | performance_history |

## 4. Variation Points

| Point | Parameter | Options |

## 5. Escalation Policy

| Primitive | Escalation Rule |
|-----------|----------------|
| assessor | Escalates if market data is stale (> 30 days) or capabilities assessment diverges from reality |
| strategist | Escalates if positioning requires capability development (the principal doesn't yet have what the market needs) |
| appraiser | Escalates if comparable pricing data is unavailable |
| negotiator | ALWAYS escalates for final terms — human performs high-stakes negotiation |
| incorporator | Escalates for any entity formation (legal action required) |
| insulator | Escalates if liability exposure is material and separation is incomplete |

## 6. Exit Conditions

- Income stabilized: OIKONOMIA reports sustainability for 60+ days from PRAXIS-generated sources
- Repositioning complete: new positioning is established and producing (no longer in transition)
- Pivoted: strategic assessment reveals current positioning is wrong; PRAXIS re-invokes from assessor with new frame
- Merged with TESSERA: when the engagement requires ongoing identity management, PRAXIS hands off relationship maintenance to TESSERA

## 7. Why PRAXIS Crystallizes Third

Once defense is stabilized (AEGIS) and economic visibility is achieved (OIKONOMIA), the bottleneck becomes income flow. PRAXIS treats the principal's economic participation as a STRATEGIC problem — not "find a job" but "what configuration of capabilities, positioning, and structure commands the highest return with acceptable risk?"

The distinction from job-seeking: PRAXIS models the field. It asks not "who's hiring?" but "what moves change my position in the field?"

## 8. INST-FORMATION Classification

**Formation type**: SYNTHESIZER (cross-configurational — combines market sensing, strategic positioning, valuation, negotiation, legal structuring, and risk compartmentalization).

**Interdependence profile**: Internal sequential with strategist as the integrative core. External: receives from OIKONOMIA (economic state), feeds TESSERA (ongoing relationships), feeds OIKONOMIA (income data back to ledger).
